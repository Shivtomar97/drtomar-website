Understanding Cancer

Cancer is a complex group of over 100 different types of diseases that can affect almost every organ in the human body. The organs in our body are made up of cells that normally divide and multiply as the body needs them. When these cells continue multiplying when the body doesn't need them, the result is a mass or growth, also called a tumor.

These growths are considered either benign or malignant. Benign tumors are non-cancerous, rarely life-threatening, and do not spread to other parts of the body. They can often be dissolved from the body by Homeopathic medicines. Malignant tumors are cancerous, can invade nearby tissues, and may spread (metastasize) to other parts of the body.

How Cancer Spreads

Cancer cells have the ability to invade neighboring tissues and organs, thus spreading the disease. It is also possible for cancerous cells to break free from the tumor and enter the bloodstream, spreading the disease to other organs. This process of spreading is called metastasis.

When cancer has metastasized and has affected other areas of the body, the disease is still referred to by the organ of origination. For instance, if cervical cancer spreads to the lungs, it is still called cervical cancer, not lung cancer.

Did You Know?

One in three people will develop cancer in their lifetime. Early detection and appropriate treatment can significantly improve outcomes.
