Understanding Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic, autoimmune skin condition characterized by patches of abnormal skin. These patches are typically red, itchy, and scaly. Psoriasis occurs when skin cells rapidly build up on the surface of the skin, forming thick, silvery scales and itchy, dry, red patches that can be painful.

The condition is not contagious but is often hereditary and believed to be related to immune system dysfunction. Psoriasis is cyclical, flaring for a few weeks or months, then subsiding for a period or even going into complete remission.

Types of Psoriasis

There are several types of psoriasis, each with distinct characteristics:

  • Plaque Psoriasis: The most common form, characterized by raised, red patches covered with a silvery white buildup of dead skin cells.
  • Guttate Psoriasis: Appears as small, dot-like lesions, often triggered by strep throat.
  • Inverse Psoriasis: Occurs in skin folds and appears as smooth, red lesions.
  • Pustular Psoriasis: Characterized by white pustules surrounded by red skin.
  • Erythrodermic Psoriasis: A severe form that can cover the entire body with a red, peeling rash.

Did You Know?

Psoriasis affects approximately 2-3% of the world's population. It can begin at any age but most commonly appears for the first time between the ages of 15 and 25 years.
